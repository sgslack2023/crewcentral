SECTION 5. BILLING

(A) Weight can be calculated in two means. 

1) Scale Tickets (Weight); Scale tickets will be provided by the carrier if the means of calculation for weight is done via scales. Some regions can be difficult to find accessible scales due to location or time of service. Scaling may be required to be done at a later time, or the carrier may chose to base the weight calculation of space take (Section 5.A.2); Calculated as; Gross Weight - Tare Weight = Charged Weight

2) Space Taken (CuFT); If the weight of the household goods will weigh less than 700 LBs per 100 cubic feet (of trailer space) taken, the billing process will automatically adjust to the calculation of 700 LBs per 100 cubic feet. Calculated as; 100 CuFT = 700 LBs (i.e. 300 CuFT Charged as : 3 x 700 = 2100 LBs Charged) 

3) The carrier reserves the right to choose the method which is best suited for the invoice. Being that if the weight is higher than the average weight over space then the means of 'Scale Tickets (Weight)' will be used for billing (and vice versa).
